About Me.

I’m Ayub Ali, a Computer Science student at Franklin University. I hold a Bachelor’s in Health Sciences and Rehabilitation from The Ohio State University, where I studied various topics including aging, integrative approaches to health and wellness, cognitive science, and disability studies, and also earned a minor in Developmental Psychology. My transition into computer science has been driven by a strong interest in technology, particularly in transformative innovations that can improve quality of life and address global health challenges. Through collaborative projects and hands-on experience, I have gained valuable insights into various aspects of the field, including front-end and back-end development. Working closely with teams on software projects has allowed me to delve into a diverse set of technologies. I have developed proficiency in front-end tools such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as frameworks like React and Dart. On the back end, I have worked with Java, Python, and Node.js, and gained experience with tools such as MySQL, Android Studio, Flutter, and GitHub. These experiences have honed my ability to create effective and user-centric applications, and I am eager to continue expanding my expertise in the software engineering domain.

  • Skills include: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, Java, React, Dart, Node.js, MySQL, Materialize CSS, Bootstrap, Android Studio, Flutter, GitHub, IntelliJ, Jupyter Notebook
